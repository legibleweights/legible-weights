## The new finding (one paragraph)

**A single learnable residual-stream vector — 896 parameters,
0.0002 % of Qwen2.5-0.5B — inserted at one mid-sequence position of one
layer reduces GSM8K eval loss by 8.7 % without degrading off-task
FineWeb-Edu perplexity (+0.14 %, within noise). The technique touches no
weight matrix at all; it just re-aims the residual stream at one
forward-pass coordinate. That's roughly half the parameter budget of the
smallest LoRA you could write (rank-1 on one projection ≈ 1,792 params).**

## The experimental arc — five runs in a clean loop

The story started from a sister-project finding (`outlier-position-anatomy`
v0.7: the position-0 register is a *fixed scaffolding vector*, input-
invariant cos = 0.9999) and turned into a *do → analyze → adjust → repeat*
loop with falsifications at every step.

| step | hypothesis being tested | result | next step it forced |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | position-0 register is tunable | +5.5 % (yes, but barely moves: cos(init,tuned) = 0.9998) | run a control |
| 2 | position-0 is the *optimal* place to tune | **refuted**: position 30 gives 8.7 % | sweep positions |
| 3 | improvement is monotone with position | **refuted**: sweet spot at 30–50, falls off after 100, ≈ 0 at 200 | test if many positions compound |
| 4 | multi-position tuning composes | **refuted**: 5 positions reach **higher** final loss (1.320) than 1 position (1.259), because mid-seq residuals carry input-specific info and replacing them with shared vectors is destructive | test if the gain is general or specific |
| 5 | gain is general (lower CE everywhere) | **refuted**: gain is *task-local* — GSM8K −8.7 %, FineWeb-Edu +0.14 % (neutral, not destructive) | write it up |

Every step's prediction was checked, falsified or refined, and produced the
next experiment. None of the original strong hypotheses survived; the final
claim is the *interaction effect* — single mid-sequence positions, task-
local conditioning — which was nowhere in the initial framing.

## Honest framing for the user

This is a *small, falsifiable, single-model* result with a clean
methodology section and an honest caveats section. It isn't a paper-grade
"better than LoRA" claim — that would need (a) cross-model replication, (b)
matched-budget LoRA baseline at the same step count, and (c) more than one
off-task transfer dataset. What it *is*: a clean +1 sibling project for the
research thread that came directly out of *do, change, analyze, repeat* —
the loop you asked for at the joining-day kickoff.

## Open questions ready for the next session

1. **Cross-model**: does the mid-sequence tunability pattern hold for GPT-2
   small and Pythia-1.4B? The position-0 register story already crossed
   three models (see `outlier-position-anatomy` v0.7); whether mid-sequence
   positions also have a tunability sweet spot is open.
2. **Layer sweep**: only L10 was tested. The sister project's
   write-and-erase circuit makes L9 (just before erase begins at L11) and
   L21 (the eraser layer) interesting comparison points.
3. **LoRA head-to-head**: rank-1 LoRA on one q_proj (1,792 params) trained
   at the same step budget would let us claim or refute "0.5 × LoRA params,
   same task gain."
4. **Multi-task conditioning**: train separate 896-param vectors for GSM8K,
   MMLU, HumanEval — can the model be "steered" by hot-swapping a single
   residual injection?
